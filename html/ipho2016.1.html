Problem 1: An Unusual Pendulum


Context: In this problem we are given a disk on a slanted plane of angle /[/theta/]. A very basic setup. But what makes it interesting is the Unusual build of the disk. Its of radius R and made of wood, but inside its curcumference, at a distance d from the axis of symmetry is a metal disk of radius r.

as you can see from the model, the disk rolls down the slanted plane for a very short period of time and then becomes still Our goal is to find the angle \[\phi\] that it rotates by before coming to a stop. Plase refer to this link for the problem statement.
The known quantities we have are the radius of the whole disk \( R \), the slanted angle \( \theta \), and the distance from the axis of symmetry to the center of the metal ball \( d \). The disk is of mass \( M \) and the ball is of mass \( m \).
To solve this problem, we first need to find the forces and then the torque acting on the disk.
From the model, it's very clear that one of the forces is the component of gravity acting between the disk and the plank: \[ F_{\text{along plank}} = mg\sin\theta \] This force will create a torque on the whole disk: \[ T_{\text{plank force}} = (M+m)mg\sin\theta R \quad \text{-----(1)} \] Because the disk's center of mass is different from its axis of symmetry, it will also experience a torque due to gravity: \[ T_{\text{gravity}} = \vec{b} \times (M+m)g = (M+m)gb\sin\phi \quad \text{-----(2)} \] Equating equations (1) and (2) gives us: \[ (M+m)mg\sin\theta R = (M+m)gb\sin\phi \] Solving for \( \sin\phi \): \[ \sin\phi = \frac{\sin\theta R}{b} \] In the problem's setup, I used \( \theta = \frac{\pi}{9} \), \( R = 4 \), and \( b = 1.75 \), and got \( \frac{51}{180}\pi \).
